Gradient, a map for everyone

Gradient is a map that visualizes the accessibility of roads ans buildings. Accessibility, or the 'gradient' information is visualized by three states/colors.

Users of Gradient

Design for the mobility impaired can develop further into design for everyone. Elevators, ramps, low floor buses were originally designed for the mobility impaired, yet now widely used by everyone. Anyone who is sensitive to graident can benefit from this service, which I believe is everyone at certain point in time. Everyone including the Wheelchair users, cyclists, children, elderly and more can use Gradient.

Accessibility Score

Accessibility score was made based on research paper, official douments, and user feedback. Roads and buildings are visualized in three colors (red - yellow - green), red being the lowest in accessibility score. Buildings were assessed based on whether they have elevators and ramps.

Navigation Coaching

Users can get navigation coaching once they input their destination. They are provided with real photos and expected time. Users can access specific information about the building including contact information.

Accessibility Campaign

Together with KUDA(Korea University Disability Association students), we made a campus map that visualizes accessibility information. The map was distributed among students and introduced in school brochure to help guide the mobility impaired students.

Collect data

I met with wheelchair users, stroller parents, cyclists and more to learn about their mobility experience. Through interviews and shadowing, I learned that there is a need for 'optimal route rather than fastest route' and that it is crucial to provide the users with accessibility information to help them move independently. I collected 'gradient' data from Seoul 3D Website(http://3dgis.seoul.go.kr/). For building data (elevators and ramps), I had to actually visit most places to find out.

"We only realize things after we get there. I'm really nervous getting to places for the first time. I have to leave at least an hour earlier than others."
"I don't want attention. I want to go to places independently."
"There are ramps that are just not usable."
"Safety of the baby is the foremost important thing. It's hard to push heavy doors or get past bumps with the baby stroller."
"The map application told me it takes 10 minutes, and then I found out it is uphill. It took me over 30 minutes."

Capture insight

The purpose of Graident became clear after research, which was to provide accessibility information to assist independent mobility experience. There was a clear need for 'optimal route not fastest'. From the user journey map, much time was wasted as the mobility impaired had to call places his/herself to get accessibilty information.

For some, it's Mt. Everest
-American Disability Association x Jeski Social Campaign
