음악을 보고 듣고 만지다, 모두를 위한 스피커

wave is speaker for everyone with which the user can watch, touch, and listen to the music. By extending the music experience from auditory to visual and tactile, Wave invites everyone including the hearing imapried to experience the music.

음악을 보다 Watch the Music

The main woofer stiumlates the liquid to form various patterns. The patterns become thinner as the sound frequency increases within the bass range. Users can play with the equalizer to witness changes in patterns.

음악을 만지다 Touch the Music

To touch the music, users can wrap their hands around the wooden body of the speaker or simply contact any part of the speaker. The dimensions and materials of the product were decided in consideration of the comfortable position to watch and touch the music.

Collect data

We met with the hearing impaired to learn about their music experience. With the help of organizations including local welfare centers and student disability support center, we could get in contact with the hearing impaired community (including sign language expert, assistive technician and more) to carry out interviews and surveys. The very first interview took place in a cafe. The interviewee had cochlear implant. We communicated both verbally and by exchaning notes. It was only later realized that cochlear implant is especially sensitive to small noise and therefore cafe is not an optimal place for an interview. So we took the next inerview in a quiet room. The interview process was new to us, yet valuable for learning about the hearing impaired. We also participated as staff in an event called, 'Concert for the Deaf', where we could observe and talk with the hearing impaired to learn about their music experience.

Capture insight

The most important insight was that, we shouldn't design for the hearing impaired, but design for everyone including them. During the interview, our user told us that she wants to enjoy music that she likes and not enjoy music that is given to her. So we changed our design direction from 'how might we design music experience for the hearing impaired' to 'how might we design music experience for everyone'

Create experience

It was an iterative process of making and then testing with the users. We developed the liquid that does not rot and leave stains on the glass cover. In order to make a working prototype, we received mentoring from a hardware expert. We tested with 3D printing and visited several woodworking shops to find the right material.
