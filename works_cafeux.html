Cafe UX 카공족을 위한 카페 디자인

Much space is wasted due to cafe visitors who put their belongings on another seat. People come to cafe to work, but often leave because they cannot find a seat. For this Cafe UX design project, we explored ways to help cafe visitors to work in cafe without seat problems.

The Table

Table needed to be both functional and provide an indrect space divider effect. The bag holders on table top serve as bag holders, but also as indirect space dividers. Also, the lamp gives light that serves as an indirect space divider between people facing each other.

The Chair

The hanger shaped shoulder rest leads people to put their coats on their chair. This bar chair can hold long coats. This groove hanger can hold bags as well.

Collect data

With various design methodology research such as ethnography, shadowing, behavior mapping, unobtrusive measures, the love letter and the breakup letter... we found many pain points within a cafe envrionment. Through affinity diagram, we found out that much pain points occur in the seating area. We then stayed for hours in cafes to observe how people behave. We drew spot maps to keep record of where people put their belongings.

Capture insight

We found out that the underlying psychological reason why people put their belonging on another seat, is because they don't want other people to sit next to them. Therefore we found their needs for a space division. We also narrowed down our scope for target user and target space.

사용자가 "감수해야죠"라고 말하는 부분에 집중하기

I believe that it is a designer's role to give cusomters more than they explicitly ask for,
and a way to do that is to pay attention to the painpoints that the user 'bears with'.

Create experience

After several idea sketches and quick prototyping, we met with people to gather feedback. We also conducted a social experiment to test the effect of indirect space divider, by placing masking tape on table top and observing how people behave. After rounds of iterative process, we developed our design.
